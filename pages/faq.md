## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Course Questions

**Q: What are the prerequisites for this course?**

A: You should have completed Java Programming (CS 1003), Object-Oriented Design (CS 2001), and Data Structures (CS 2010). If you haven't taken these courses, please speak with the instructor about whether you're ready for this course.

**Q: Do I need to purchase any software?**

A: No! All required software for this course is free:
- Android Studio (free IDE from Google)
- Java Development Kit (free)
- Git and GitHub (free accounts)

**Q: Is there a textbook?**

A: No required textbook. All course materials are freely available online through the official Android Developer documentation and course resources.

### Technical Setup

**Q: What computer hardware do I need?**

A: A modern laptop or desktop with:
- At least 8 GB RAM (16 GB recommended)
- 20 GB free disk space
- Windows, macOS, or Linux
- Internet connection

**Q: Can I use a Mac?**

A: Yes! Android Studio runs on Windows, macOS, and Linux equally well.

**Q: Can I develop Android apps on a Chromebook?**

A: Chromebooks have limited Android development support. A traditional laptop is strongly recommended.

**Q: Do I need an Android phone?**

A: No. Android Studio includes an emulator that simulates Android devices. However, testing on a real device is beneficial.

**Q: How do I install Android Studio?**

A: See the [Resources]({{ '/pages/resources/' | relative_url }}) page for the Android Studio download link and follow the official installation guide.

### Course Policies

**Q: What is the late policy?**

A: Assignments submitted late will receive a 10% penalty per day, up to 5 days maximum. After 5 days, work will not be accepted.

**Q: Can I submit assignments early?**

A: Yes! Early submissions are always welcome.

**Q: What is the attendance policy?**

A: Attendance is mandatory. More than 3 unexcused absences will impact your grade.

**Q: How do I request accommodations for a disability?**

A: Contact your university's disability services office. They will work with the instructor to provide appropriate accommodations.

### Grading & Assessment

**Q: How is my grade calculated?**

A: 
- Participation: 5%
- Homework: 15%
- Assignments: 20%
- Projects: 40%
- Final Project: 20%

**Q: What is a passing grade?**

A: A grade of 70% or higher (C or better) is passing.

**Q: Can I retake assignments?**

A: No, assignments must be submitted by the deadline. However, you can meet with the instructor during office hours to discuss improvements.

**Q: How often should I expect to be graded?**

A: Assignments are typically graded within one week of submission. Projects may take up to two weeks.

### Android Development Questions

**Q: What version of Android should I target?**

A: We'll target Android 10 (API 29) as minimum and Android 13 (API 33) as target. Check course materials for any updates.

**Q: Should I learn Kotlin or Java?**

A: We'll use both! The course starts with Java and introduces Kotlin midway through. Both are important for Android development.

**Q: What's the difference between an Activity and a Fragment?**

A: Activities are full-screen components; Fragments are reusable UI pieces within an Activity. You'll learn the details in Week 4.

**Q: Where can I find more Android development help?**

A: Check the [Resources]({{ '/pages/resources/' | relative_url }}) page for links to Stack Overflow, official documentation, and more.

### Troubleshooting

**Q: Android Studio won't start. What do I do?**

A: 
1. Make sure Java JDK 17+ is installed
2. Try deleting the .gradle folder in your home directory
3. Reinstall Android Studio
4. Contact the instructor if problems persist

**Q: My app keeps crashing. How do I debug?**

A: Check the Logcat in Android Studio for error messages. The [Resources]({{ '/pages/resources/' | relative_url }}) page has debugging guides.

**Q: The emulator is very slow. What can I do?**

A: Enable virtualization in your BIOS settings (for Windows), or use Android Studio's hardware acceleration features.
